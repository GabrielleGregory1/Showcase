Creating a website to showcase the artork of CU Students, Alumni and Professors..

The digital design program and the broader College of Arts and Media currently lack a centralized, cohesive digital presence to showcase the exceptional work of their students and alumni. This limits the program's ability to gain public visibility, attract prospective students, and connect students and alumni with industry professionals. According to UCraft, creating a place to showcase your work helps boost your online presence. Furthermore, having your work online points to the value of community in the university and can inspire other creatives. Despite the wealth of talent within the program, this lack of public visibility means missed opportunities to promote student work, foster alumni connections, and enhance the col-lege’s reputation in a competitive arts and design landscape. Students, alumni, and faculty need a centralized, professional digital showcase for their work because it is critical for gaining public visibility, connecting with industry professionals, and attracting prospective students to the program.

User Goals

  • To help students/alumni gain exposure to industry professionals and recruiters by showcasing their digital portfolios
  • To learn about what kind of projects were created each graduation year
  • To see the growth of artists after graduation.
  • To see what topics and types of work were created and explored in the CAM program.
  • To recruit potential students to the school and program.
  • For students to understand what type of work professors have done in their own professions

User Groups

  • Current art and design students / alumni seeking to showcase their work to peers, instructors, and the broader com-munity.
  • Instructors looking to assess and promote student work, as well as administrators needing to present the program’sachievements
  • Employers, recruiters, and industry leaders seeking to discover emerging talent for internships, jobs, or collaborations.
  • Individuals considering enrolling in the program who want to explore the caliber and style of student work.
  • Art lovers and community members interested in viewing and engaging with the creative output of students/alumni

Design Rationale

Through sketches of wireframes, device rationale, competitive analysis and research, design I designed multi-screen, multi-device site. I emphasized and prioritized the screens that are where the students, alumni and professors can showcase their artwork and profile. I figured this should be priority in showcasing the site because that is what makes the site different. I want there to be different ways to go about the artwork from different classes, to hashtags, along with an area for Thesis work to be showcased. I wanted the site to be mostly visual with every opportunity to show artwork on a screen possible without being too cluttered.
